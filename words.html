Convergence

Okay, then, let's assume that the community has 30 people, with 10 each of the greedy "Greedy", the lowest price "Honest Man", and the reasonable "Ordinary People". Let's see how the composition of contributors changes over time ...

1. Voting
Once a month, the community holds a workload vote

2. Eliminate inefficiencies
After each vote, eliminate the five types with the lowest input-output returns

3. Increase efficiency
After each vote, add five types of people with the highest input-output return

... Let us try it →

Now, let's start the "Vote - Eliminate - Add" 3-step dance and repeat it several times!

We use the benefit-output ratio as a measure of elimination, always eliminating the 5 people with the lowest benefit-output ratio and increasing the 5 people with the highest benefit-output ratio

Obviously, the first to be eliminated is "Greedy"...

Then, "Greedy" were all out ...

Following closely behind, "Honest Man" also slowly increased the "fees" they applied for due to the low returns, and turned themselves into "Ordinary People" ...

Finally, the results show ...

... "Ordinary People" stayed.

In the short term, "Greedy" and "Honest Man"will stay in the group for a while, but eventually we will see that when they are punished, "Greedy" will be restrained and "Honest Man"will realize the need to be more efficient.

As a result, you will see the members of the team gradually reach a consensus, and eventually evolve into the same kind of person.

Above, reveals the three things we need to master in order to get as many votes as possible.



1. Make "Price" as small as possible
You need to know that voting is a "Two for one" process, and that a higher quality Results is better for the same price.


2. Get "Results" as much as possible
When you have a lot of results, you can declare more prices, and each price is a vote.


3. Be as "Efficient" as possible
Results as the only reference for the community, of course, the more efficiently completed, the higher the return!


We are all ordinary people, we all have a greedy side, we sometimes want to be lazy; but when their "results" and "price" will be made public, "human nature" will jump out and start to play, and then ... convergence works.

Similarly, if someone is punished or earns less than expected, it will drive people towards the "three things", and the temptation of higher returns will drive people to be more efficient in order to produce more "results".

Of course, real-world is affected by much more than this. There's fraud, difficulty in quantification, understanding bias, blah blah blah.
